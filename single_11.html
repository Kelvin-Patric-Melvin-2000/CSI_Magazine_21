COMPUTER SCIENCE - Science or Art?

by GOUTHAM PRAKSASH

Computer Science, whenever mentioned, is usually associated with science, math and logic. Creativity or art never comes into conversation. But what do you see when you look at never ending lines of code in your editor? It is a bunch of gibberish if you don’t know programming but if you do and you are good at it, you start to see patterns, some that make sense and others that don’t, but they are patterns nevertheless, and we usually associate patterns with art. Let us consider another scenario where you are trying to figure out a solution to a programming question and look up its answer online, and when you find it you are awestruck by its implementation. What you are essentially doing here is admiring the way the code works or admiring the beauty of it. Computer Science is basically you trying to choreograph a dance with a bunch of 1s and 0s, even if you are using some complex JavaScript framework or writing a low level Assembly language code. If you ask me one does not need a very high IQ or knowledge to understand or appreciate an algorithm, all you need is a creative and artistic mind.

One can create a very basic code that prints a pattern on your console or make a beautiful website by just having fun with these 1s and 0s. Complexity of what you are doing isn’t important here, whether it is big or small, it is art. That’s why even if you aren’t in this field it is always fun to learn coding and other tools related to computer programming, it fries your brain and at the same time makes you think creatively. It is never too late to get into computer science, if you are 13 and over, you can get into this wonderful “topsy-turvy” world of art and science at any given time and start having fun. Look at all the beautiful algorithms or take a glimpse at those beautiful websites and start appreciating it more because now you would know how much effort goes into it. Closing your eyes and visualising an algorithm is always fun, and you need not be an expert to do it either. The nature of computation is most likely the nature of the universe. The laws of “logic” and "thought" that the computer uses are the same laws of thought that a human being uses. Well in the end, writing this also makes me wonder if there is a bit of philosophy hiding in there too. At the end of the day, it is just a machine and a human working together to solve a problem or two.

If these two paragraphs from me inspired any of you to code, I am a happy man!
