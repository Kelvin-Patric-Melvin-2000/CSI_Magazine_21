END OF BLACK HOLE INFORMATION PARADOX

by VAISHNAVU BAIJU

According to Einstein’s general theory of relativity, when anything enters a black hole the laws of physics break down due to the black hole singularity where the space time fabric curves steeply inwards and nothing escapes after that.

In the 1970s Stephen Hawking took a semi- classical approach to black holes. Using quantum theory and Einstein’s theory he proved that black holes do emit radiations and will eventually evaporate but , still according to Hawking if you fall into a black hole , all information of your particles will be lost But Don Page pointed out that Hawking’s theory violated a central principle of quantum mechanics called unitarity, which says that the present always preserves the information about the past. So there came a contradiction about the loss of information due to blackholes. This is the black hole information paradox.

According to Page if a person falls into a black hole, that person won’t be gone for good, particle by particle the information needed to reconstitute the body would re-emerge. The key to understanding why the information will be preserved lies in a process called quantum entanglement.

When black holes emit radiation that radiation maintains a quantum mechanical link to its place of origin, if we try to measure the radiation or the black hole separately, the information would look random but when we look at it together, they exhibit a pattern Page said in theory that if we measure the entanglement entropy of the black hole, we will see that some information will escape the black hole. And it was later studied by physicists and found to be true. But many other variables are yet to be found, but from Page’s theory we now know for sure information is not truly lost when it enters a black hole.
