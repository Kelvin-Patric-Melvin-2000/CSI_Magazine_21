3D PRINTING OF STRUCTURES - THE NEW ERA OF CONSTRUCTION?

by Adith D Jayakrishnan

The idea of printing is something which dates back centuries. The world’s first commercially viable printing press was developed by Johannes Gutenberg back in 1440 A.D, compared to this 3D printing or otherwise the idea of bringing a virtual image into the real world only started in the 1940’s and it took another forty years in the 80’s for man-kind to have commendable development in this technology. Initially, 3D printing was just considered as a technology to build prototypes but today we have reached a situation where we are exploring its hidden potential and one among them is building structures using 3D Printing.

These structures are built by inputting the virtual structure in a computer program which is connected to the 3D printer. A mechanical structure similar to a column is used as a base onto which a special mix of concrete made of cement, sand, silt and coarse aggregate is poured using the robotic arm of the 3D printer.

3D printed structures require fewer laborers; however it requires a skilled labor force having sufficient knowledge in the fields of computer science, electrical and civil engineering. There is practically a little or no wastage of materials used for constructing structures via 3D printing compared to conventional construction methods. 3D printed structures require less time to build compared to conventional methods of building structures.

The main issue of 3D printed structures is that this technology is at its infant stage. Buildings are something which requires a huge lifespan and this technology basically reimagines the way we construct structures, so we don’t have enough citations about the reliability and durability of these structures. However, results obtained so far seem promising and show us the great potential this technology has.

India is a country having low productivity in construction. Often most construction projects are delayed in our country due to various reasons. On top of that infrastructure in India is limited and it is not enough to hold the huge population we have. So, we have to move into new technologies which are cost-effective and reduce construction time and this technology seems to be a promising solution to many of our problems.

Adapting new technologies is always necessary for man-kind to grow and develop. Many technologies which may seem infant or irrelevant may become the new convention and history has always proven that this is true. 3D printing of structures definitely appears to be one among them.
