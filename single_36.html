CANCER VACCINES

by SWATHY SURESH M

Cancer is one of the most dreaded diseases affecting human beings and is a major cause of life loss for mankind. The innovative developing technology can prevent cancer and save millions of lives indeed and will be a great contribution. Nothing is more important than saving lives.

Cancer is an abnormal growth of cells which tend to proliferate in an uncontrolled way and in some cases metastasize (spread) to other parts of the body. The cancer cells continue to divide giving rise to masses of cells called Tumors. Tumors are of two types Benign and Malignant. Benign tumors normally remain confined to their original location and do not spread while malignant tumors on the other hand are a mass of proliferating cells called neoplastic or tumor cells. These cells grow very rapidly, invading and damaging normal tissue. Cells sloughed from such tumors reach distant sites through blood and new tumors are formed. This property is called Metastasis and it is the most feared property of malignant tumors.

Cancer is the second leading cause of death as per the World Health Organization (WHO). Globally Cancer is responsible for the death of nearly 9 million people. As per the studies in India, we see 1300 deaths due to cancer every day. Vaccines have proven effective in preventing diseases caused by viruses and bacteria for a long time. Cancer vaccines can prevent healthy people from getting certain cancers caused by viruses.

There are two types of vaccines that prevent cancer approved by the US Food and Drug Administration (FDA). HPV Vaccine: The vaccine protects against the human papillomavirus (HPV). If the virus stays in the body for a long time, it can cause some type of cancer. The FDA has proved HPV vaccine to prevent Cervical, Vaginal and Vulvar cancer, Anal cancer, Genital Warts. Hepatitis B vaccine: This vaccine protects against the Hepatitis B virus. This virus can cause liver cancer.

There are vaccines that treat existing cancer called Therapeutic Vaccines. These Vaccines are a type of cancer treatment called Immunotherapy. They work to bust the body’s immune system to fight cancer.

Doctors give treatment vaccines to people who already have cancer. These vaccines can keep the cancer from coming back and can also destroy any cancer cells still in the body after the treatment ends. Cancer treatment vaccines boost the immune system’s ability to find and destroy antigens. Some cancer vaccines are personalized. This means that they are made for just one person. This type of vaccine is produced from samples of the person's tumor that are removed during surgery. Even though Cancer vaccines are effective there are many challenges to be solved so as to make it available for millions of people around the globe.

The latest advances in cancer treatment have created a whole new outlook on how to treat this deadly disease. These advances have developed from a deeper understanding of the molecular basis of cancer. It is a great achievement of technology that a vaccine is developed against this dreaded disease. It is a gift to the world and we may hope for better results in the future.
