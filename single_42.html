Keeping your Data safe

by Anagha S Menon

The Covid-Lockdown situation has brought us to a place where we do everything online. From grocery shopping to attending classes, we are heavily dependent on the internet to complete all our day-to-day activities. In such a period, data privacy has become more important than ever before.

It's not just companies who have to keep their data safe, it's each one of us. As individuals, our data online could cost us a lot. Falling to phishing schemes and identity thefts are just some examples. Most people rely on the false comfort that "This happened there, to someone else. It's not going to happen to me." This can only be called denial, because you could easily be the next. Your data is just as exposed as theirs' was. Complete online privacy is pretty much unattainable, but there are some steps we can take. Some of them are explained below.

  • Keeping different unique, secure passwords. (Sites are available to check your password strength). An easy tip is to use phrases and a mixture of symbols, numbers and alphabets in both upper and lower cases.
  • Do not overshare on social media sites. This is an accidental mistake we all tend to make, share only what needs to be shared. Try to avoid sharing personal details like date of birth, location, travelling plans, etc.
  • Private Browsing. Your computer saves your browsing history, temporary internet files, cookies, etc. To avoid this, one thing you can do is to do your web surfing in private mode. Web browsers today offer their own versions of this type of privacy protection. For example, a.) The Incognito Mode in Chrome, b.)The Private Browsing setting in Firefox, and c.)The InPrivate Browsing in the Internet Explorer. When you search with these modes turned on, others can't trace your browsing history from your computer. The drawback here is that these private modes aren’t completely private. When you’re searching in incognito or private mode, your Internet Service Provider (ISP) can still see your browsing activity. The websites you use can also track you.
  • Anonymous search engines and virtual private networks can bolster your online privacy.
    Using a different search engine: Most of us use Google as our go-to search engine. However, these days people prefer anonymous search engines and the main reason for this is privacy. These search engines don’t collect or share our search history, clicks, etc. Anonymous search engines can also block ad trackers on the websites you visit. Example: DuckDuckGo.
    Using a virtual private network: A virtual private network (VPN) gives you online privacy and anonymity by creating a private network from a public internet connection. VPNs mask your Internet Protocol (IP) address so your online actions are virtually untraceable.

    Make sure that you are using a VPN when you’re on public Wi-Fi at a library, cafe, railway station or any other public location. A VPN will make it very difficult for cybercriminals to breach your online privacy and access personal information.

    Tip: Try to use a service from a trusted security provider to get the maximum amount of privacy protection while online.

  • Beware of each click. One of the simplest ways in which hackers compromise our online privacy is through phishing. Scammers often try to trick you into providing valuable personal/financial information, in very realistic ways. Fake emails and websites are used. The most common ones are emails that ask you to click on a link and verify your financial information to keep your account from being frozen or closed. Do not fall for these scams! Clicking on a phishing link, takes you to a spoofed web page and sends the information you enter to the wrong kind of people.

    Tip: Before clicking on any links, hover your cursor over the link and view the destination URL. If it doesn’t match the website you use, don’t click.

  • Secure your mobile devices. We need to make an effort to protect our online privacy on our phones and tablets, as much as on our computers/laptops. Always use a passcode to lock your phone. Even though it's a hassle to unlock your phone every time you want to use it, this passcode can give an extra layer of protection if your phone is lost or stolen. Make sure your passcode is complex. Tip: Don't share this passcode with too many people.
  • Be careful with your downloads and updates. Download things using only the legitimate sources. Do not ignore software updates. These updates often include important protections against the latest viruses.
  • Using quality anti-virus software. Make sure to install anti-virus software on all your devices. This software can protect you from hackers to some extent. Tip: Install their updates as soon as they become available.

Doing all this still doesn't guarantee complete protection. However, these simple steps can go a long way in protecting yourself. Just remember that if you don't protect yourself, no one else will!
